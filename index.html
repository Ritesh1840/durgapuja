About Durga Puja

Durga Puja is a major Hindu festival celebrating the victory of Goddess Durga over the buffalo demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil. It is widely celebrated in West Bengal, Assam, Odisha, Bihar, and other parts of India, with grandeur and devotion.

The festival spans over five days—Shashthi, Saptami, Ashtami, Navami, and Dashami—each marked with rituals, prayers, and cultural events. People dress in traditional attire, participate in cultural performances, and visit beautifully decorated pandals (temporary structures) that house the idols of Goddess Durga.

The History of Durga Puja

Durga Puja has deep historical roots. The festival was first celebrated in the late 16th century by the landlords of Bengal as a private event. However, it was in the 18th century when the festival became popular among the masses, especially during the rule of the British East India Company.

The first public or 'Barowari' Durga Puja was organized in Kolkata in 1790, allowing people from all social backgrounds to come together and celebrate. Over the centuries, Durga Puja has grown into a grand cultural celebration, becoming not only a religious festival but also a community event showcasing art, culture, and social unity.
